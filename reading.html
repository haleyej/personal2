Basically Everything I've Read In The Past 2 Years That's Worth Mentioning (in progress!)

Mostly an archive for me, but hopefully it provides inspiration to others!

Books

  • Bodies on the Line by Lauren Rankin
    • Really good history of clinic escorts and clinic defenders! Ranking does a great job historically situating the pro-choice movement. Written pre Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health, so some of the chapters, particularly the last one, felt like watching a slow moving car crash
  • Eileen by Ottessa Moshfegh
    • The first 200 pages and like whatever but then the last 50 are INSANE. If you enjoy a slow-build or stories with unexpected twists, then I highly recommend
  • Thanks for Everything (Now Get Out) by Joseph Margulies
    • This was kind of meh. The author profiles the Olneyville neighborhood of Providence, RI but in my opinion it spends too much time on the case study and not enough on the general point about gentrification and displacement that the author is trying to make
  • The White Album by Joan Didion
    • What can I say about Joan Didion that hasn't already been said? I coincidentally read this right before moving out to California, which felt very on-the-nose
  • Trick Mirror by Jia Tolentino
    • An instant classic, one of my favorites of all time
  • Normal People by Sally Rooney
    • The hype is real and the hulu series is just as good
  • Invisible Women: Data Bias in a World Designed For Men by Caroline Criado Perez
    • This book was so underwhelming and just threw out random statistics about data bias against women without really saying anything. I'm always floored when people recommend this
  • Norweigan Wood by Haruki Murakami
    • The writing is so beautiful that I honestly forget that it's lowkey a little sexist while reading it
  • Bad Blood by John Carreyrou
    • No matter how crazy tyou think the Theranos saga was, I promise it's crazier. Great investigative reporting and a suprisingly quick read
  • The Cult of We: WeWork, Adam Neumann, and the Great Startup Delusion By Eliot Brown & Maureen Farrell
    • Another fantastic journalistic take-down of a start up. I honestly think this is better than Bad Blood but this book is like 400 pages long. The authors do a great job connecting WeWork's downfall to broader issues in the start-up world and venture capital
  • Cultish: The Language of Fanatacism by Amanda Montell
    • This book isn't as "deep" as my usual non-fiction reads, but it goes quickly and it's very interesting

Articles

  • A Very Dangerous Place to Be Pregnant Is Getting Even Scarier
    • Fantastic investigation into maternal health in rural Texas
  • Learning and Not Learning Abortion
    • Really insightful essay about how abortion is increasingly not taught in U.S. medical schools, often for political reasons
  • A Rhodes scholar barista and the fight to unionize Starbucks
    • Incredible story and also self explanatory from the title
  • Unhinged 'Transvestigators' Think They're the Only CIS People Left
    • Deep dive into a bizzare corner of the internet
