About Me

I am orginally from Manistee, Michigan, a quiet town on the shores of Lake Michigan. I am firmly convinced Northern Michigan is one of the greatest places in the country.

I never thought I'd become a data scientist (when I started college I wanted to be a political science major), but I quickly learned I love statistics and using computational methods to model interesting phenomena.

I'm primarily interested in data and computation as a tool to understand peoples behavior at scale, even if all nuiance and messy-ness of real life can never fully be captured by numerical abstractions. I'd love to work in public-interest technology or civic technology one day.
